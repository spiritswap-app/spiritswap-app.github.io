SpiritSwap on Fantom and SPIRIT token

SpiritSwap is a decentralized exchange on the Fantom Opera network. The app supports token swaps, liquidity pools, farms, a bridge interface, and inSPIRIT locking. SPIRIT is the protocol token used for incentives, governance-related participation, and locking into inSPIRIT. The official site describes SpiritSwap as a Fantom DeFi hub rather than just a single swap screen, which matches real search demand around SPIRIT farms, inSPIRIT rewards, bridge routes, and Fantom token liquidity. Users should treat current TVL, volume, and SPIRIT market data as live metrics because Fantom DEX activity changes quickly.

SpiritSwap fees and best swap rates

SpiritSwap swap costs include Fantom gas, pool fees, and any price impact from the selected trading pair. The official site says its AMM architecture combines an upgraded UniV2-style AMM with stable swap functionality, which is intended to improve rates across volatile and stable assets. For real users, the quoted output is more important than a generic fee claim: thin Fantom pools can create slippage, especially for long-tail tokens. Searchers comparing SpiritSwap fees should check route, liquidity depth, token contract, and price impact before confirming a swap.

SpiritSwap farms and inSPIRIT rewards

SpiritSwap farms reward liquidity providers with SPIRIT and connect to the inSPIRIT locking model. The official site says SPIRIT can be used to invest, vote, boost farm rewards, and participate in governance. inSPIRIT is tied to three value propositions described by the project: boosted farm emissions, a share of revenue from DEX swaps, and governance participation through voting and proposals. Farm APRs are not fixed because they depend on emissions, pool liquidity, token prices, and trading fees. Users should compare gross APR with impermanent loss and token exposure.

SpiritSwap bridge to Fantom

SpiritSwap includes a bridge interface intended to help users move assets into Fantom from other chains. The official site describes SpiritBridge as a bridge aggregator for routes from chains such as Ethereum, Avalanche, Terra, and Arbitrum, though available routes and providers can change. The important workflow is to bridge the correct asset to Fantom, keep enough FTM for gas, and then swap or provide liquidity on SpiritSwap. Bridge users should verify destination chain, wrapped asset type, minimum received amount, and bridge provider before signing.

SpiritSwap FAQ

What is SpiritSwap used for?

SpiritSwap is used to swap Fantom-based tokens, provide liquidity, farm rewards, bridge assets into Fantom, and lock SPIRIT into inSPIRIT. It is a Fantom DeFi hub rather than a custodial exchange. Users connect a wallet, approve token spending, and interact with smart contracts directly.

What is SPIRIT on SpiritSwap?

SPIRIT is the SpiritSwap protocol token. It is used in farming incentives and can be locked into inSPIRIT for boost, revenue-sharing, and governance-related functions described by the protocol. Like other DEX tokens, SPIRIT's market price and liquidity can be volatile, so token exposure matters when farming or locking.

Does SpiritSwap only run on Fantom?

SpiritSwap is primarily a Fantom Opera DEX. The protocol's core swap and farm activity is centered on Fantom, though its bridge interface helps users move assets from other chains into Fantom. Users should make sure their wallet is on Fantom before trying to trade or provide liquidity.

How do SpiritSwap farms work?

SpiritSwap farms reward users who deposit liquidity pool tokens. The pool earns swap fees when trades occur, and farms may distribute SPIRIT incentives. Returns vary by pool volume, liquidity, emissions, token price, and impermanent loss. A high displayed APR can fall quickly if token prices move or more liquidity enters.

What is inSPIRIT?

inSPIRIT is the locked SPIRIT position used in SpiritSwap's boost, reward-sharing, and governance model. The official site describes inSPIRIT as giving participants farm-emission boosts, a share of DEX swap revenue, and voting participation. Locking reduces liquidity, so users should understand lock duration and opportunity cost first.

Is SpiritSwap audited?

The SpiritSwap site references audits and links audit-related repositories. Audit coverage can reduce some smart-contract uncertainty, but it does not remove all risk. Users still need to consider token approvals, bridge risk, pool liquidity, price impact, farm emissions, and the possibility of interacting with fake tokens or duplicate domains.
